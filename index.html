Systems Of Two Linear Equations

In mathematics, we can (not always!) take two linear equations and determine their intersection point. But how will we do that? Let's find out.

Let's first take a look at the general form. (It's not that bad, I promise!)

\( \begin{cases} ax + by = c \\ dx + ey = f \end{cases} \)

Not that bad, right? a, b, c, d, e, f can be any integer. Let's take a look at an example and try to solve it in different ways.

\( \begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ x + 2y = 7 \end{cases} \)

Scared? Don't be. We will beat that problem and gain some knowledge here. Let's go to the first method.

The Substitution Method

If you take a look at the first equation, we can arrange it so that:

\( y = 5 - 2x \)

We can "plug" in the value of \( y \) to solve for \( x \) now.

\( x + 2(5-2x) = 7 \)

\( x + 10 - 4x = 7 \)

\( -3x + 10 = 7 \)

\( -3x = -3 \)

\( x = 1 \)

We also need to find \( y \), so we can plug in 1 to solve for \( y \).

\( y = 5 - 2(1) \)

\( y = 3 \)

The solution is \( (1, 3) \).

Let's move on to the second method.

The Elimination Method

When you are playing a game, "eliminate" usually means removing a player from the game. In math, it's exactly the same, just with variables.

Let's say you have an ex (get it?) and you want to eliminate him/her. You can do that in math. Watch and learn.

\( \begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ x + 2y = 7 \end{cases} \)

Look at the second equation. We can multiply it by 2 so it becomes:

\( \begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ x + 2y = 7 \end{cases} \)

You can multiply the second equation by 2.

\( \begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ 2x + 4y = 14 \end{cases} \)

Subtract the second equation from the first equation. You will find that x is gone.

\(-3y = -9\)

\(y = 3\)

Now, plug the value of y to the first/second equation (up to you!)

\(2x+3 = 5\)

\(2x = 2\)

\(x = 1\)

We find the same solution, which is (1, 3).

Both method works and are equally good, so choose whatever you want!

If you have a graphing calculator/software, you can also graph the equations and find the intersection.
